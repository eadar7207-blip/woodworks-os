# LinkedIn Strategy for Eitan Adar

**Build credibility and inbound leads via authentic positioning as agent + automation specialist.**

---

## Positioning Statement

> "Building automation for real estate teams who want to scale without hiring. Currently shadowing agents to understand their problems deeply, then designing solutions that actually work. Becoming a licensed agent to prove it."

**Why this positioning:**
- **Authentic** — Eitan IS shadowing agents, WILL become an agent
- **Specific** — Not "I do automation" (vague), but "for teams trying to scale" (clear)
- **Credible** — "Shadowing agents" shows respect for the craft, not arrogance
- **Vulnerable** — "Learning" + "becoming an agent" shows humility, builds trust
- **Action-oriented** — "Designing solutions" suggests competence, not just theory

---

## Target Audience Profile

**Primary:** Team leads (5-15 agents trying to scale)
- Pain point: Can't hire fast enough to handle growth
- Salary: $150K-300K+ (have budget for solutions)
- Openness: High (actively looking for ways to scale)
- Buying cycle: 4-12 weeks (not urgent, but interested)

**Secondary:** Individual agents (overworked, want to be more efficient)
- Pain point: Spending 20+ hours/week on admin
- Salary: $75K-200K (budget varies)
- Openness: Medium (open if solution is cheap/easy)
- Buying cycle: 8-16 weeks (need to convince ROI)

**Tertiary:** Real estate coaches selling to their students
- Pain point: Students ask "how do you stay organized?" — no good answer
- Salary: $100K-300K (high-value segment)
- Openness: High (looking for tools to teach students)
- Buying cycle: Fast (if solution is productizable)

## Content Pillars (5 Core Topics)

### Pillar 1: Automation Patterns
**What:** Specific workflows that can be automated (with examples)
**Why agents care:** "Oh, I do that manually. I could automate it?"
**Format:** Post patterns, show before/after, keep it concrete
**Examples:**
- "This team was spending 6 hours/week on lead follow-up. Here's what they automated."
- "5 workflows every growing team is doing manually"
- "The most underrated automation: [specific workflow]"

### Pillar 2: Agent Pain Points
**What:** Problems agents have but don't talk about
**Why agents care:** "YES. That's me. How do I fix it?"
**Format:** Vulnerability + honesty. Share what you learned shadowing
**Examples:**
- "I shadowed an agent who spends 2 hours/day on emails that could be templates"
- "Team leads hate this: [specific pain]. Here's why it happens."
- "The #1 problem growing teams face (that nobody talks about)"

### Pillar 3: Real Estate + AI
**What:** How AI can help without replacing agents
**Why agents care:** Agents are worried about AI taking their job
**Format:** Reframe AI as tool (helps agents win), not threat
**Examples:**
- "AI isn't replacing agents. It's replacing the admin work agents hate."
- "How my first client used AI to [specific outcome]"
- "[Company] is using AI to automate [workflow]. Here's why agents should care."

### Pillar 4: Behind-the-Scenes Learning
**What:** Shadowing stories, lessons learned, "what surprised me"
**Why agents care:** Authentic learning creates connection
**Format:** Personal narrative. Raw honesty. What I learned today.
**Examples:**
- "I shadowed an agent today and learned her #1 productivity killer. It's not what I expected."
- "3 things nobody tells you about being a real estate agent"
- "This team member's job will be obsolete in 2 years (and she knows it). Here's what she's doing about it."

### Pillar 5: Case Studies / Wins
**What:** Real before/after. How much time agents saved. What they can now do instead.
**Why agents care:** Proof that automation works (vs. theoretical)
**Format:** Numbers. Testimonial. What changed for the team.
**Examples:**
- "This team saved 120 hours/month. Here's how they spent the time instead."
- "[Agent name] was working 60-hour weeks. After automation, 45 hours. Here's what else changed."
- "One small automation gave this team $50K in new revenue"

## Execution Details

### Posting Format

**Text posts (70% of posts):**
- **Hook line:** First 2 lines = stop scroll (ask question, make bold statement, show surprise)
- **Body:** 150-300 words. Concrete. Examples > theory.
- **Call to action:** Soft. "What's your biggest bottleneck?" or "What do you think?"
- **Hashtags:** 3-5 relevant. #RealEstate #AgentAutomation #RealEstateTech

**Example post:**
```
I shadowed a team yesterday. One agent spent 90 minutes forwarding emails from prospects to the transaction coordinator.

90 minutes. Every single day.

When I asked why it wasn't automated, she said: "We tried once. It was complicated. Gave up."

This is the #1 pattern I'm seeing: Teams WANT to automate. They just don't know where to start, or they tried once and failed.

That's changing.

What's the one task you spend the most time on that feels like it could be automated?

#RealEstate #Automation #TeamProductivity
```

### Link Strategy

**Where to link:**
- LinkedIn profile (headline, about section)
- Website (if you build one — not necessary yet)
- Calendar link (for "let's talk" CTAs)
- Wiki (for detailed case studies, if public)

**What NOT to do:**
- Don't spam with links
- Don't use link shorteners (LinkedIn algorithm penalizes)
- Don't link to landing pages in first 20 posts (build credibility first, then convert)

### Engagement Strategy

**Reply to comments:**
- Reply to every comment in first 4 hours (algorithms reward speed)
- Ask follow-up questions (encourage conversation)
- Keep replies 1-2 sentences (don't hijack comments section)

**Engage with other agents' posts:**
- Follow 10-15 RE agents in your niche (team leads, growing teams)
- Comment thoughtfully on their posts (2-3x per week)
- Build relationships (future partners, clients, referrers)

**DM strategy:**
- After someone engages with 3+ posts, send thoughtful DM (not sales pitch)
- "Hey, I noticed you commented on [post]. You mentioned [specific pain]. I've been thinking about this a lot. Would love to chat — no pressure."
- This converts 5-10% of engaged followers into conversations

## Common Pitfalls to Avoid

❌ **Don't be generic**
- "I help real estate teams automate" (boring)
- ✓ "I'm learning RE by shadowing, then building automations for what I see" (specific)

❌ **Don't oversell**
- "My automation doubled this team's revenue" (unproven, claims)
- ✓ "This team saved 20 hours/week and reinvested it into outreach" (specific, believable)

❌ **Don't post infrequently**
- Algorithm punishes inconsistency
- ✓ Commit to 2-3x per week minimum (even if small posts)

❌ **Don't ignore comments**
- Posts that get comments but no replies = algorithm thinks it's not engaging
- ✓ Reply within 4 hours, keep conversations going

❌ **Don't sell too early**
- "DM me if you want automation" in first 10 posts = everyone mutes you
- ✓ Build credibility for 20-30 posts, THEN softly invite conversations
