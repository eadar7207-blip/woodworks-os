# Chicago Prospect Analysis: Lakefront vs Wicker Park

| Factor | Lakefront Realty | Wicker Park Homes |

| **Team Size** | 5 (all full-time) | 5 (4 part-time) |

---

## Chicago Lakefront Realty

### Deep Dive

**Strengths (for us):**
- Full-time team = stable, consistent revenue
- High volume ($200M) = can afford premium pricing
- Growing (added 2 agents) = already investing in scaling
- Lincoln Park/Lakeview = affluent market, higher deal values
- Decision-maker (Sarah) is broker = more autonomy

**Challenges (for us):**
- Broker experience = may have seen automation before
- Larger team = harder to get buy-in from all 5 agents
- Growth stage = might want to "grow organically" before spending
- May already have CRM solution in place

**Pain Level:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐ (High)
- 75 hours/week of admin work across team
- $37,500/week opportunity cost

**Best Pitch Angle:**
> "You're growing (added 2 agents). Next hire shouldn't be a person — it should be automation handling follow-up. Frees your agents to focus on closing."

**Buying Timeline:** 3-4 weeks (Sarah will want to see proof)

**Objections to Prepare For:**
- "We're growing fine without it"
- "Can't we just hire an admin?"
- "Our agents like working independently"

**Contact Strategy:**
1. Email Sarah (decision-maker) with growth angle
2. Follow up with 3-day check-in
3. Offer 30-min discovery call (no pitch)
4. Send 2 case studies of similar teams

## Wicker Park Homes Group

### Deep Dive

**Strengths (for us):**
- First-time buyer focus = high need for nurture sequences
- Part-time agents = less overhead, more open to outsourcing
- Growth-focused (James) = faster decision-maker
- Tech-savvy team (using Slack) = easier to adopt automation
- Lost lead problem is quantifiable = easy ROI calculation

**Challenges (for us):**
- Smaller volume ($150M) = lower revenue per deal
- Part-time team = less stable (agents might leave)
- Newer tools = might be DIY-minded
- Could scale without us if they hire admin

**Pain Level:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (Very High)
- First-time buyers need hand-holding
- 8 lost leads/month = $140K/year lost
- Buyer communication is broken

**Best Pitch Angle:**
> "First-time buyers go cold without consistent contact. You're leaving $140K/year on the table with lost leads. Automation guarantees no buyer falls through cracks."

**Buying Timeline:** 2-3 weeks (James is decisive)

**Objections to Prepare For:**
- "Can't we just text them more?"
- "Our agents prefer personal touch"
- "How much does this cost?"

**Contact Strategy:**
1. Email James with lost lead + ROI math
2. Follow up with specific leads they're losing
3. Offer 15-min quick call (super low-friction)
4. Send video demo of buyer nurture sequence
5. Mention: "You're growing, so this scales with you"
