# Shadowing Plan: Sohail Real Estate Group

## Objective

Shadow Sohail or one of his agents to:
1. Understand their actual workflow (not theoretical)
2. Identify automation opportunities firsthand
3. Build genuine relationship before pitching
4. Gather proof points for your solution

## What to Look For

### Lead Follow-Up (CRITICAL)

- [ ] How many leads do they contact per day?
- [ ] What tools do they use? (email, text, phone, CRM?)
- [ ] How long does follow-up take?
- [ ] Do they have a system or is it ad-hoc?
- [ ] What leads fall through the cracks?
- [ ] How many follow-up touches before they give up?

**Document:** Time spent on follow-up, tools used, missed opportunities

### Scheduling & Calendar

- [ ] How do they schedule showings? (email, phone, app?)
- [ ] Do conflicts happen? (double-bookings, confusion?)
- [ ] How do they sync with other agents?
- [ ] Time wasted coordinating?

**Document:** Scheduling pain points, number of back-and-forths per showing

### CRM & Data

- [ ] What CRM do they use? (eXp tools? Personal?)
- [ ] How do they track client info?
- [ ] Where do notes live? (email, CRM, sticky notes, phone notes?)
- [ ] Do they lose information?

**Document:** Data organization challenges, info silos

### Communication with Clients

- [ ] How often do they touch clients?
- [ ] What channels? (text, email, call, app?)
- [ ] Consistent messaging or scattered?
- [ ] Time spent on communication vs selling?

**Document:** Communication patterns, gaps in follow-up

### Team Coordination (if shadowing with Sohail/group leader)

- [ ] How do agents coordinate?
- [ ] How does Sohail track team performance?
- [ ] Do agents follow the same process or all different?
- [ ] Pain points in scaling the team?

**Document:** Group coordination challenges, visibility into agent activity

---

## Questions to Ask

**Casual (Don't be obvious):**
- "What takes up most of your time each day?"
- "What part of the job do you hate?"
- "How do you keep track of all your leads?"
- "How many times do you follow up before moving on?"
- "How do you coordinate showings?"

**If They Mention Pain:**
- "Have you tried [tool]?"
- "What would save you the most time?"
- "How much time does this take you?"
- "Does your whole team do this the same way?"

**Don't Pitch Yet.** Just ask and listen.

---

## What You're Hunting For

**The Golden Quote:**
Something they say like:
- "I spend 10 hours/week on follow-up"
- "I lose leads because I forget to follow up"
- "Scheduling is a nightmare"
- "I wish I could clone myself"
- "If only I had 10 more hours in a day"

**These are your sales hooks.**

---

## During the Shadow

### Take Notes On:
- **Time:** Note when they're doing admin vs selling
- **Frustration:** When do they get frustrated?
- **Bottlenecks:** Where does time get lost?
- **Tools:** Screenshot their tools/process (if possible)
- **Quotes:** Write down exact pain statements
- **Volume:** How many leads/showings/deals?

### Build Rapport:
- Be genuinely curious
- Help where you can (fetch coffee, assist with paperwork)
- Ask good questions
- Show interest in their business
- Don't sell — just be present

### Get Contact Info:
- Ask for email/phone
- Connect on LinkedIn before/after
- Make it natural (not transactional)

---

## After the Shadow

### Day 1 (immediately after):
- Write down everything you noticed
- Organize by pain point
- Identify top 3 automation opportunities
- Draft specific value prop for them

### Day 3:
- Send follow-up message (LinkedIn or email)
- Reference something specific you observed
- Mention: "I noticed [pain point], I have an idea..."
- Ask for 15-min call

### Day 7:
- Call/meeting to pitch solution
- Use observed pain points as proof
- Show how automation fixes their specific issue
- Don't oversell — just solve their problem

## Success Metrics

By end of shadowing:
- [ ] Identified top 3 pain points
- [ ] Have their contact info
- [ ] Have specific quotes about pain
- [ ] Know their tools + workflow
- [ ] Have ideas for 2-3 automation opportunities
- [ ] Have plan for follow-up conversation

This isn't cold outreach. This is earned trust + specific knowledge.
